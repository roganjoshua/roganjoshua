About Colin

Colin Cameron was crippled in a serious road accident at the age of nine in 1974. Being subjectively positioned in this way has given him a disturbed but interesting perspective on the world.

Having been patronised and condescended to by more non-disabled tossers than he cares to remember has given him a sharp eye for the stupidity of life. In his work he explores ideas around normality and abnormality, the grotesque and the ideal, impairment and disability, anger, oppression, violence, resistance, conformity and the ridiculousness that conformity requires.

He has recently been identifed and confirmed as a Stuckist by Paul Harvey and Charles Thomson.

He lives in Whitley Bay and is happy to admit that technically he really has very little idea about what he is doing when he paints.
